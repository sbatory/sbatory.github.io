rtwage — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 10, 2026

rtwage is a real-time earnings tracker. It is designed to work entirely on your device: there are no user accounts, no sign-in, and we operate no servers that receive your data.

Data you enter stays on your device

Everything you put into rtwage — savings accounts and rates, jobs, wages, schedules, punches, stock holdings, tax settings — is stored only on your iPhone (in the app's local storage, shared with its widget). We cannot see it. It leaves your device only when you choose to export a backup file, and that file goes wherever you send it.

Network requests the app makes

To show live market and reference data, the app fetches from the following public data providers. These requests contain only what is technically necessary (for stock quotes, the ticker symbols you track). They never include your name, balances, wages, or any other personal data — the app has none to send.

  • Yahoo Finance, Nasdaq, Cboe — stock prices and dividend information for tickers you add
  • FDIC — the national average savings rate shown for comparison
  • Frankfurter (ECB reference rates) — currency exchange rates when you display a non-USD currency

As with any internet request, these providers technically receive your IP address. Their own privacy policies govern their services.

What we don't do

  • No analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting SDKs
  • No advertising and no ad identifiers
  • No tracking across apps or websites
  • No selling or sharing of data — we never possess it in the first place

TestFlight betas

If you use a beta version through Apple's TestFlight, Apple may share standard beta feedback and crash logs with us as described in Apple's privacy policy. These contain diagnostic information, not the financial data you enter in the app.

Deleting your data

Use Settings → Reset all data in the app, or simply delete the app — everything it stored goes with it.
