SlugMenu — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

SlugMenu shows UCSC dining hall and cafe menus, hours, and nutrition information. It is designed to work entirely on your device: there are no user accounts, no sign-in, and we operate no servers that receive your data.

Data you enter stays on your device

Everything you set up in SlugMenu — favorite dishes, allergen and dietary preferences, station ordering, appearance settings — is stored only on your iPhone. We cannot see it, and it is never uploaded anywhere.

Network requests the app makes

To show menus and hours, the app fetches public information from UC Santa Cruz websites. These requests contain nothing about you — no name, no preferences, no identifiers; the app has none to send.

  • nutrition.sa.ucsc.edu — daily menus, prices, and per-dish nutrition labels published by UCSC Dining
  • dining.ucsc.edu — live opening hours and seasonal closures

As with any internet request, these sites technically receive your IP address. UCSC's own privacy policies govern their services. Fetched menus are cached on your device for up to 7 days to reduce network use.

What we don't do

  • No analytics, telemetry, or crash-reporting SDKs
  • No advertising and no ad identifiers
  • No tracking across apps or websites
  • No selling or sharing of data — we never possess it in the first place

TestFlight betas

If you use a beta version through Apple's TestFlight, Apple may share standard beta feedback and crash logs with us as described in Apple's privacy policy. These contain diagnostic information, not your in-app preferences.

Deleting your data

Delete the app — everything it stored goes with it. Cached menu data also expires automatically after 7 days.
